Turning Rookies into Daily Players

The Insight: While analyzing cohort data for a fantasy sports product, I noticed a brutal pattern — 95% of new users churned before Day 30. The industry was pouring money into acquisition, but the real lever was retention. I dug deeper through user interviews and session replays and uncovered three core anxieties killing first-time engagement: choice overload (too many options, no guidance), fear of losing money (real-money contests on Day 1), and lack of domain knowledge (users felt they weren't "expert enough" to play).

My Approach: I reframed the product strategy from "compete on prize pools" to "build confidence before asking for commitment." I designed a three-pillar intervention targeting each anxiety:

  • Smart-Start Onboarding: I introduced a guided, risk-free first contest — auto-drafted teams with simple choices so new users could experience the thrill of playing without the paralysis of building a team from scratch.
  • AI-Powered Coach: I specced and drove development of an in-app coach that surfaced data-driven player recommendations. This eliminated the "I don't know enough" barrier and gave users confidence in their picks.
  • Progression Engine: I designed a daily missions and streak system to create a habit loop — giving users a reason to return even on non-match days, which was critical for long-term retention.

The Outcome: By tackling the root causes of churn instead of papering over them with discounts, I was able to fundamentally shift the product's retention curve. The approach outperformed industry benchmarks and validated a key thesis: reducing user anxiety is a higher-leverage growth strategy than increasing prize incentives.

Personalized Training for Every Athlete

The Insight: I studied Strava's engagement data and noticed a clear pattern — users training for endurance goals (marathons, century rides, triathlons) were Strava's highest-value segment, but the platform offered them no structured path to their goal. They logged activities religiously, yet had to rely on external apps or generic plans for actual training guidance. This was a retention risk: power users were splitting their workflow across competitors that offered structured plans.

My Approach: I identified an opportunity to turn Strava's biggest data asset — years of individual activity history — into a personalized training engine. The core hypothesis: if we can generate adaptive plans rooted in a user's own performance data, we can capture the full training workflow and deepen platform lock-in. I mapped out the product strategy across three pillars:

Product Design Decisions:

  • Goal-Based Adaptive Plans: I designed the system to generate plans calibrated to a user's current fitness level — whether they're training for a first 5K or a sub-3:30 marathon. Plans adjust weekly based on actual performance vs. targets.
  • Cross-Sport Integration: Rather than treating running, cycling, and strength as siloed activities, I pushed for a holistic model that accounts for cross-training load — a key differentiator over single-sport competitors like TrainingPeaks.
  • AI-Powered Coaching Layer: I scoped an intelligent coaching surface that flags overtraining risks, suggests recovery windows, and recommends workout swaps — turning raw data into actionable guidance users could trust.

The Outcome: This feature closed a critical gap in Strava's product offering by transforming passive activity logging into active training guidance. It strengthened the case for Strava Premium by giving subscribers a tangible, personalized reason to stay — directly improving subscriber retention and plan completion rates.

📊 Success Metrics I Defined:

  • Adoption Rate: % of eligible users who start a training plan within 30 days of launch
  • Completion Rate: % of users who finish their plan vs. abandon mid-way
  • Performance Uplift: Measurable pace/power improvement across plan participants
  • Retention Lift: Premium subscriber churn reduction among plan users vs. control
